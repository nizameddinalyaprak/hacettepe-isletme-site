Frequently Asked Questions

1) What is the maximum number of ECTS credits I can take per semester?
Students in four-year programs can take up to 30 ECTS credits in the first, second, third, and fourth semesters, and up to 40 ECTS credits from the fifth semester onwards. Courses taken from other universities are also included in these limits.
2) What is the maximum duration to complete the program?
Students must complete their undergraduate programs within a maximum of seven years, regardless of whether they registered each semester, excluding the one-year foreign language preparatory class.
3) Which courses must I complete to graduate?
Students must successfully complete common compulsory courses, field-specific compulsory courses, and both field-specific and non-field elective courses.
4) What are the common compulsory courses?
Turkish Language (TKD104), Atatürk's Principles and History of Turkish Revolution (AİT203, AİT204), and Basic Information and Communication Technologies Usage (BEB650) are common compulsory courses in all higher education programs.
5) What are the compulsory courses ING111, ING112, ING211 and ING212? Can I be exempted?
These courses aim to develop language skills in 100% English programs. They are offered by the School of Foreign Languages. For prerequisite and exemption details, please visit ydyo.hacettepe.edu.tr.
6) What are the compulsory courses?
These are courses marked with "Z" (compulsory) in the Business Administration undergraduate curriculum.
7) What do field-specific and non-field elective courses mean?
Field-specific: Elective courses within your own program. If the quota is full, you may choose another course.
Non-field: Courses offered by other departments, taught in English or a foreign language. Details: secmeli.hacettepe.edu.tr
8) How many ECTS credits must I complete to graduate?
A total of 240 ECTS must be earned: 160 ECTS compulsory, 80 ECTS elective (up to 10 ECTS can be non-field). At least 70 ECTS of field-specific electives must be completed.
9) How can I take courses from other universities?
You must apply to the department for equivalency before the semester begins. Courses are recorded on the transcript upon board approval.
10) Is attendance mandatory?
Yes, 70% attendance is mandatory. Students with more than 30% absenteeism are not allowed to take exams. For repeated theoretical courses where the attendance requirement was previously met, attendance is not required.
11) How much does the final exam contribute to the overall grade?
The final exam contributes between 40% and 60% of the overall grade. The exact ratio and exam conditions are announced by the instructor at the beginning of the semester.
12) How is the make-up exam grade evaluated?
Make-up exam results are evaluated together with the student's mid-semester performance.
13) When should I retake a failed compulsory course?
A failed compulsory course must be retaken in the first semester it is offered and must be passed before graduation.
14) Can I take another course instead of a failed elective?
Yes, you have the right to withdraw from an elective course (up to 3 courses). A different elective can be taken in place of the withdrawn course. Special provisions may apply for graduating students.
15) Can I retake courses I have already passed?
Passed courses (except A1 and A2) can be retaken to improve the GPA. The most recent grade is the effective one.
16) How many types of exams are there? Is the midterm mandatory?
There are six types of exams: midterm, final, exemption, three-course, make-up, and excuse. At least one midterm exam is mandatory for each course.
19) What are the requirements for the three-course exam?
Students who have at most 3 remaining courses for graduation or who have passed all courses but have a GPA below 2.00 can apply. Exams are held on dates specified in the academic calendar.
20) What are the graduation requirements?
1) Complete 240 ECTS (160 compulsory, 80 elective),
2) Maintain a minimum GPA of 2.00,
3) No failed courses remaining.
