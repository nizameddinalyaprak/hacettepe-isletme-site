Undergraduate Program

Purpose

Our undergraduate program, which has been accepting students since 1976 and delivering education in English since 1990, is grounded in nearly half a century of accumulated knowledge and experience. With a structure that brings together diverse disciplines such as Law, Accounting-Finance, Organizational Behavior, Marketing, Quantitative Methods, Production Management, and Management-Organization, our program aims to deliver a dynamic and contemporary education. Its fundamental goal is to cultivate individuals who are capable of critical inquiry, eager to learn, and open to experience.

The discipline of Business Administration holds an important place in the rapidly changing and increasingly competitive nature of the business world. From this perspective, one of the most important objectives of our department is to prepare students to adapt to different conditions, not shy away from competition, take initiative, and exercise leadership. The academic education provided in the department lays the foundation for this. Additionally, enhancing our students' communication skills, developing a spirit of teamwork and solidarity, expanding their problem-solving capacities, and instilling innovative thinking are among the primary goals of our curriculum.

Program Structure

The duration of our undergraduate program is four years, excluding the preparatory year. Our program consists of 65% compulsory and 35% elective courses. All courses to be taken in the first two years are compulsory. These courses cover areas that form the foundation of business studies—such as management, organizational behavior, marketing, and accounting—as well as statistics, mathematics, and law, providing students with a general perspective, way of thinking, and theoretical framework. In the third year, five out of twelve required courses are elective. Core disciplines such as finance and production management are compulsory, while elective courses are available in human resources, sales and marketing, quantitative methods, and law. In the final year, eleven out of twelve courses are elective. Numerous elective courses are offered across all the above-mentioned fields. In this way, as our students approach graduation, they can select courses aligned with the industries, sectors, and fields they plan to work in, and gain the opportunity to develop expertise in those areas.

While compulsory courses ensure students develop theoretically and conceptually, elective courses prioritize the evaluation of current and global topics, events, and phenomena. Case studies, presentations, company analyses, group projects, and simulations are the most commonly used methods in elective courses. The content and delivery of our elective courses play a significant role in developing our students' social skills and creating a distinguishing advantage.

Student Exchange Programs

Our department is one of the most active departments in the Erasmus+ program at our university. With bilateral agreements with more than twenty European universities, our program regularly sends students abroad and accepts students from abroad. Additionally, two other student exchange programs are operated within our department: Mevlana (international) and Farabi (national).

For more information about the Erasmus+ exchange program, please visit the Erasmus page under the exchange programs tab.

Internship Opportunities

Although there is no mandatory internship program in our department, students are encouraged to undertake internships. For this purpose, two elective courses are offered each academic semester: MAN 327 for third-year students and MAN 437 for fourth-year students. Students who complete an internship during the summer period can enroll in these courses and gain the opportunity to evaluate the similarities or differences between the theory and practice of business from an academic perspective.

For more information about internships, please visit the internship tab.

Alumni

Our alumni are our source of reputation, pride, and joy. When examined in terms of professional profile, we observe that our graduates work across a remarkably wide spectrum. We have alumni working in various private sector fields including banking, insurance, auditing, human resources, sales, marketing, project management, and import-export. Looking at the public sector, we see that our graduates hold important positions at prestigious institutions such as the Central Bank, Capital Markets Board (CMB), Banking Regulation and Supervision Agency (BRSA), the Treasury, and various ministries. In addition to those working in the public and private sectors, we should also note that many alumni have ventured into entrepreneurship and successfully established their own businesses. Furthermore, we encounter our department's graduates in fields such as media, public relations, culture and arts, healthcare, education, sports, and tourism.
